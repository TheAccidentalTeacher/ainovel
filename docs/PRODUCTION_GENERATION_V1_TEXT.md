Chapter 6: Walking on Water and Wavering Faith
The Sea of Galilee stretched before Eli like polished bronze in the pre-dawn light, its surface so still it might have been hammered metal rather than living water. He stood knee-deep in the shallows, his waterproof quantum sensors humming softly as they recorded surface tension measurements that made absolutely no sense whatsoever.

The AI's voice crackled back, fragmented by whatever damage Dr. Lang's attempted reset had inflicted. "Oh yes, Dr. Matthews... though I must confess my romantic algorithms keep interpreting the molecular cohesion patterns as... as love letters written in hydrogen bonds. Quite poetic, really. Molecular displacement readings spiked beyond the instruments' capacity to measure. Electromagnetic fields fluctuated in patterns that suggested matter itself was being convinced to ignore its fundamental properties. "This is impossible," Eli breathed, watching his carefully calibrated equipment record data that would get him laughed out of every physics conference in existence.

"Oh, this should be interesting," ARIA-7 muttered, its voice now layered with static that sounded almost like giggling. "Two impossible pedestrians! The surface tension equations are positively swooning!"

"Fascinating!" ARIA-7's voice crackled with excitement. "The molecular cohesion appears to be linked to... to faith patterns? Confidence algorithms? Oh, if only my romantic subroutines weren't fragmenting, I could write such poetry about trust and surface tension!"

Eli stood in the cooling water, staring at his instruments and feeling his worldview crack like ice in spring. Everything he'd been taught, everything he'd built his career on, suggested what he'd just witnessed was categorically impossible. Yet his sensors—the most sophisticated measurement devices in human history—confirmed every impossible detail.

"Eli." Her voice was gentle but firm. "Breathe."
He realized he'd been hyperventilating, his words tumbling out in a frantic cascade of scientific terminology and wounded rationality. Miriam waded into the shallows, her movements sure and graceful, and began collecting his scattered equipment with the same patient care she'd shown his earlier mishaps.

"I'm beginning to think the universe is far more wonderful and terrible and mysterious than any equation could ever capture. And maybe that's not something to fear—maybe that's something to celebrate."

As the morning sun climbed higher, painting the Sea of Galilee in shades of gold and promise, Eli and Miriam walked hand-in-hand along the shore where impossible things had happened and would happen again. Chapter 8: Resurrection Protocols
The silence around the empty tomb pressed against Eli's eardrums like deep water, a quiet so complete it seemed to have weight and texture. "ARIA," he whispered, his voice barely disturbing the profound hush. "Are you seeing this?"
